About Waikawau Bay

A beautiful bay in the North-East Coromandel Peninsula, New Zealand saved from development for over fifty years.

“Waikawau Bay: One of the jewels of the Coromandel ... Apart from the campground at the southern end, this beach has no development on or next to it. Instead you can experience a fantastic long sandy ocean beach with all the ecosystems that a dune environment should have!" - Colville Guiding Service

50 Years of Protection from Development

Let’s continue the legacy and safeguard this treasure for the future

  • 1960s

    The first threat

    A Waikato developer wanted to build 2,000 houses and a shopping centre behind the dunes of this remote beach. They were stopped, and went to Pauanui instead.

  • 1970s

    Beach Purchase

    Environmental activists ( the Coromandel Physical Environment Society) and the Environment Council successfully campaigned to have the beach bought by the Department of Lands and Survey. The Environment Council wrote to the then Minister of Lands, concerned that the county council “would be likely driven by financial opportunities of a subdivision.” These words ring as true today.

  • 1990s

    The golf course

    An owner of nearby land applied to build 10 houses and an 8-hole golf course at the North headland of the beach. Locals mounted a successful campaign and the stopped the development.

  • 2003

    The North Headland

    Land at the the north end of Waikawau Bay was again under threat. 150 hectares were bought up through the Nature Heritage Fund and added to the land already owned by the Department of Conservation.

  • Recently

    The South Headland

    Land at the the south end of the beach had a subdivison consent approved without notification! The diggers are in.

  • Today

    Save Waikawau Bay Campaign Launched

    Time to get back to work to keep this amazing bay a tresure for us and future generations to enjoy.

Waikawau Bay is Under Threat

Did you know there’s a subdivision being built on 60ha at the south end of Waikawau Bay?

Bulldozers have been in and cleared native bush for house sites and roads. Nobody was notified.

A bay is a bay - an entire bay; you can't have half a bay deemed as outstanding and protected, while the other half becomes scarred and carved up for development.

They are bulldozing as we speak. It’s a race against time.

News & Media

News

On Saturday April 23rd, 100 people turned up to celebrate Waikawau Bay! A memorable day in the long history of the protection of Waikawau Bay.

We celebrated 50+ years of protection from subdivision and ongoing conservation projects at Waikawau Bay.

It was such a gorgeous day in every respect, families relaxing, children playing, friends catching up, talking about how we can save this iconic bay from encroaching subdivision at the South end, overlooking the beach.

What's happening with the bulldozers?

Lots of you have asked where the subdivision is. The subdivision land is on the south side of the bay. It joins up with the DoC camp site and runs parallel to the Matamataharakeke Track. On a map it’s marked as 891 Tuateawa Road. The developer has begun on the top of the hill overlooking Waikawau Bay. This is just the beginning though - unless we stop them.

Who are we?

We’re the Waikawau Bay Guardians a group formed recently to save Waikawau Bay from development and to continue the 50 year long history of protecting this outstanding wilderness beach for future generations.

The aim of the group is to keep Waikawau Bay as the last remote beach on the Coromandel peninsula accessible by road and we are exploring ways to ensure we can protect this land from development.
